Our Purpose

This website has the downloadable visual and written resources referred to in the book. They are intended to be used as an adjunct to support the therapeutic work of the SUSI intervention and not a treatment in themselves.

These resources have been designed by workers in SLAM1 but are available in an editable form so that professionals can make their own changes to make the handouts applicable to the families they are working with.

The handbook describes the clinical assessment and intervention in detail, bringing to life the emotional worlds and communications of babies and very young children in their early caregiving relationships.

1 South London and Maudsley (NHS Trust)

SUSI Summary

What is SUSI?

To recap, SUSI is a parent-child assessment and intervention for Under 5’s. The main features are:

  • A comprehensive assessment model (two sessions) to identify a child’s social-emotional capacity and development with their primary caregivers.
  • Use of standardised assessments and clinical observations.
  • Tailored approach to focus on central issues affecting the parent-child dyad.
  • Brief intervention, 6-15 sessions depending on clinical needs.
  • Themes for intervention and how to focus work.
